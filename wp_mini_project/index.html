Water Facts You Should Know

Understanding the importance of water helps us appreciate why conservation matters

🌍

Global Water Distribution

Only 3% of Earth's water is freshwater, and only 0.5% is available for drinking. The rest is locked in glaciers, ice caps, and underground.

🚿

Daily Water Usage

The average person uses about 135 litres of water per day. In India, urban households use approximately 150-200 litres daily per person.

🔧

Leaky Pipes Matter

A dripping faucet can waste more than 11,000 litres per year. That's enough water for over 180 showers!

🌱

Agriculture Impact

Agriculture accounts for 70% of global freshwater use. It takes about 2,500 litres of water to produce just one kilogram of rice.

⚡

Energy Connection

Heating water accounts for about 18% of home energy use. Conserving hot water saves both water and energy.

🏠

Indoor vs Outdoor

About 30% of household water is used outdoors. In dry climates, outdoor use can account for up to 60% of total consumption.

Water Conservation Tips

Simple changes can make a big difference. Explore tips for every area of your life.

⏱️

Shorter Showers

Reduce shower time by 2 minutes to save up to 40 litres per shower. Use a timer to stay on track.

💧 Saves: ~1,200 litres/month
🚿

Low-Flow Showerhead

Install a low-flow showerhead that uses 7.5 litres per minute instead of 20 litres.

💧 Saves: ~1,900 litres/month
🦷

Turn Off the Tap

Don't let water run while brushing teeth or shaving. Turn it off and save litres daily.

💧 Saves: ~750 litres/month
🚽

Check for Leaks

Add food coloring to your toilet tank. If color appears in the bowl without flushing, you have a leak.

💧 Saves: ~3,800 litres/month
🛁

Fill Tub Wisely

When taking a bath, plug the drain immediately and adjust temperature as it fills.

💧 Saves: ~200 litres/month
🍽️

Full Loads Only

Run your dishwasher only when it's full. This is more efficient than hand-washing dishes.

💧 Saves: ~1,200 litres/month
🥬

Wash Produce Wisely

Wash fruits and vegetables in a basin rather than under running water. Reuse the water for plants.

💧 Saves: ~570 litres/month
🧊

Thaw Responsibly

Thaw frozen foods in the refrigerator overnight instead of using running water.

💧 Saves: ~380 litres/month
🚰

Install Aerators

Faucet aerators can reduce water flow by 50% while maintaining pressure.

💧 Saves: ~1,060 litres/month
♻️

Reuse Cooking Water

Save water from boiling pasta or vegetables. Once cooled, use it to water plants.

💧 Saves: ~230 litres/month
🌅

Water Early Morning

Water your lawn early in the morning to reduce evaporation. Avoid watering on windy days.

💧 Saves: ~1,900 litres/month
🌿

Use Mulch

Apply mulch around plants to retain moisture and reduce watering needs by up to 50%.

💧 Saves: ~2,840 litres/month
🌧️

Collect Rainwater

Install rain barrels to collect water from gutters. Use it for watering plants and gardens.

💧 Saves: ~4,900 litres/month
🌵

Choose Native Plants

Plant drought-resistant and native species that require less water to thrive.

💧 Saves: ~3,800 litres/month
🚗

Skip the Hose

Use a bucket to wash your car or visit a car wash that recycles water.

💧 Saves: ~380 litres/wash
📊

Monitor Your Bill

Track your water usage monthly. Unusual increases may indicate leaks or waste.

💧 Awareness is key!
🔧

Fix Leaks Immediately

A small drip can waste 75 litres per day. Check faucets, toilets, and pipes regularly.

💧 Saves: ~2,270 litres/month
🏆

Buy BIS/ISI Certified Products

Look for Bureau of Indian Standards certified water-efficient fixtures and appliances.

💧 20% more efficient
📚

Educate Your Family

Teach children about water conservation. Make it a family effort and celebrate progress.

💧 Multiply your impact!
🤝

Spread the Word

Share conservation tips with neighbors and community. Collective action creates real change.

💧 Community impact!
